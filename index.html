John McCarthy

(September 4, 1927 - October 24, 2011)

  • Computer Scientist
  • Public Speaker
  • Professor
Never abandon a theory that explains something until you have a theory that explains more.

1927

On September 4th, 1927, John McCarthy was born in Boston, Massachusetts. He was born into a communist family living in the great depression

1944-1951

John graduated from Belmont High 2 years early in 1944. He was accepted into Caltech and skipped 2 years of mathematics and recieved a Bachelor award in 1948. He moved to Princeton and recieved a PhD in 1951.

1955-1962

In 1955, John became a professor at Dartmouth after studying in Stanford and Princeton. A year later, he moved to MIT as a reaserch educator. After years of studying, John became a permanent professor at Stanford

1971-2011

John continued to study mathematics and artificial intelligence, where his AI discoveries awarded him the Turing Award in 1971. He recieved many awards later before his retirement in 2000 and his passing in 2011

Awards

- Turing Award 1971 (Association for Computing Machinery)
- Kyoto Prize (1988)
- National Medal of Science (US) in Mathematical, Statistical, and Computational Sciences (1990)
- Inducted as a Fellow of the Computer History Museum "for his co-founding of the fields of Artificial Intelligence (AI) and timesharing systems, and for major contributions to mathematics and computer science" (1999)
- Benjamin Franklin Medal in Computer and Cognitive Science from the Franklin Institute (2003)
- Inducted into IEEE Intelligent Systems' AI's Hall of Fame (2011), for the "significant contributions to the field of AI and intelligent systems"
- Named as one of the 2012 Stanford Engineering Heroes
